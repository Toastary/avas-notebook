			+ Red herring
				+ A red herring is a distraction used to divert attention away from the main issue, argument, or, in literature, the true culprit. It often acts as a misleading, irrelevant, or false clue intended to confuse or redirect.
			+ Hasty Generalizations
			+ Straw-man arguments
				+ A straw man argument is an informal logical fallacy where someone misrepresents, exaggerates, or fabricates an opponent's argument to make it easier to attack. Instead of addressing the actual, more nuanced position, the speaker defeats a flimsy "straw" version. This tactic distracts from the real issue and avoids genuine debates.
			+ Category errors
				+ A category error is a logical blunder where properties belonging to one logical category are mistakenly assigned to another or when an object is placed in the wrong conceptual class.
					+ Short analogy: A person is shown all the classrooms, labs, and teachers at a university, and then asks "But when do I get to see the University".
						+ The error: They are mistakenly treating the "University" as another physical object in the same category as the buildings, rather than understanding it as a the abstract organization of those buildings.
