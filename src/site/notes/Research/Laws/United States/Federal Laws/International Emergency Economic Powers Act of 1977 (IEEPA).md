# Law Summary

+ *__Congress:__* https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R45618
	+ The International Emergency Economic Act was enacted in 1977, the statute gives the President the authority to regulate economic transactions.
		+ As the executive, this law granted the branch the authority/control over international investment, migration, and communications between the [[Research/Library/Geography/United States\|United States]] and its enemies
# Relevance

+ In 2026, the Supreme Court (SCOTUS) decided to hear the case of Learning Resources, Inc. v. [[Research/Library/People/Donald Trump\|Donald Trump]]
	+ The goal of the case was to establish a very clear, objective interpretation of what the law/statute(s) granted president [[Research/Library/People/Donald Trump\|Donald Trump]]
		+ [[Research/Library/People/Donald Trump\|Donald Trump]] as the executive officer of the [[Research/Library/Geography/United States\|United States]] justified unilateral broad tariffs on other countries. While at the time it was known that the authority to impose tariffs was mostly given to congress since tariffs acts as a tax on imported goods
			+ The president does have some authority to impose tariffs on other countries under Section 232 of the [[Research/Laws/United States/Federal Laws/Trade Expansion Act of 1962\|Trade Expansion Act of 1962]] the statute within itself doesn't grant the president unilateral authority to impose tariffs though they can without congress, they must follow the proper process
				+ The Secretary of Commerce must initiate investigation, and has 270 days to submit a report to the President. Then, if the Commerce finds a threat, the President has 90 days to decide if they concur with those findings, if the President agrees, they can then implement tariffs or quotas under Section 232
