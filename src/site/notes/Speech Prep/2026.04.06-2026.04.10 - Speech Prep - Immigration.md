## Speech Prep

Since the 1980s, the U.S. witnessed a surge in immigration this includes both legally, and illegally. In todays generation we have politicians debating about a prominent solution on what to do in regards to the large illegal immigration within the U.S., according to DHS there are over 11-15 million "undocumented" migrants. What most politicians won't tell you is that most of these "undocumented" immigrants were once legal, they came over in the U.S. legally once but overstayed their legal stay, I'm pointing this out because most of the argument surrounding illegal immigration is that they actually cross over the border "illegally". People often find illegal migration to be a societal issue, both economically, and socially, the claims are often that undocumented immigrants are a net negative to the tax payers, in reality in a federal level undocumented immigrants are fiscally a net positive to government revenue
