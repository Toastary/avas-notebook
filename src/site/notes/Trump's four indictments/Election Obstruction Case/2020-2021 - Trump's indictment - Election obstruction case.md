			+ After a presidential election, the ascertainment is submitted by the governor of each state, (and by the mayor of the District of Columbia) to the Archivist of the [[Research/Library/Geography/United States\|United States]] and others
			+ The certificates must bear the state seal and the governor's signature. Each state is free to choose the appearance and layout of the certificate. Staff from the Office of the Federal Register ensure that each certificate contains all legally required information
			+ Each of the seven original certificates must meet the following five requirements:
				+ List the names of the electors chosen by the voters and the number of votes each received
				+ List of the names of all other candidates for elector and the number of votes each received
				+ Be signed and dated by Executive (signatures by auto-pen or stamp are not permitted)
				+ Carry the State seal
				+ Contain at least one security feature, as determined by your State, for purposes of verifying the authenticity of such certificate
